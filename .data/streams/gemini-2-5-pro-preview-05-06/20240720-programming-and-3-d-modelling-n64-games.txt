## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Level Design (Course 13):** Includes a "snowman path" and "snowman cave" requiring players to dodge attacks, and a large slide over an abyss with a planned helix and ice block jump, followed by a wallkick section.
- **Control Precision:** The game features significantly increased control precision (65,336 angles vs. Mario 64's 4096), which Kaze notes will make existing M64 strategies behave differently. This was done for speed (polynomial for arctan/sine/cosine) but also increased accuracy.
- **Memory Management:** A major challenge was running out of memory due to a large slide design. This was solved by implementing a "whole memory allocation thing," significantly improving performance.
- **Asset Optimization:** Ongoing efforts to optimize assets for performance, such as reducing materials and polygons on models provided by Biobak (e.g., Womps, Janitor Koopa).
- **Enemy Behavior:** Kaze works on refining enemy AI, such as the Wiggler's pathing and attack animations, and fixing bugs related to their behavior and appearance.
- **Camera Adjustments:** Kaze considers and implements camera adjustments to improve gameplay on features like the slide, weighing options like scaling the slide or tilting the camera.
- **Technical Bottlenecks:** Identifying and addressing performance issues like RSP bottlenecks and excessive scheduler run times.
