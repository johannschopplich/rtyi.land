## Game Development Process
- Kaze is actively developing a "space level" or "Cosmic Colony" (also referred to as a "plantation" or "space greenhouse"), focusing on platforming mechanics, visual polish (textures, glow effects, signs), and level layout.
- The development process is iterative: core platforming is established first, and then aesthetic elements and narrative justifications are built around it ("Really what we're doing now is we already made the platforming, and now we're just trying to justify the platforms we've made.")
- There's an expectation of a later "debug" phase where much of the current work will be reworked or refined.
- Story development is secondary to gameplay in the initial stages: "We haven't really planned the story yet. I think it's better to just put together some gameplay first, and then plan the story around the gameplay you have."
- Performance on actual N64 hardware is a consideration, with Kaze testing builds on console and noting FPS ("43 FPS. Ooh, it doesn't like all these plants here.")
- Work involves using tools like Fast64 (a Blender add-on).
- Specific features being implemented/refined include:
    - Bumpers for movement.
    - Low-poly crops.
    - Signs for guidance.
    - Glowing outlines for platform visibility.
    - Skybox implementation.
    - Star collection animations (blinking).
    - Textures for various surfaces (platforms, water tanks).

## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Visual Clarity:** A recurring challenge is ensuring platforms and interactive elements are clearly visible to the player. Solution example: "I'm going to have glowing outlines on all the platforms in The End."
- **Level "Feel" / "Sauce":** Kaze strives to make levels feel good to play, referring to a "sauce" that a level might initially lack but gains through iteration.
- **Technical Bugs:** Encountering and addressing game crashes, such as one related to Backward Long Jumps (BLJs): "My fucking game crashed. ... It's clearly something in the PLJ code itself causing an infinite loop somewhere." He later seems to fix it or understand it better.
- **Performance Optimization:** Ensuring the game runs well on N64 hardware, especially with many objects on screen ("Ooh, it doesn't like all these plants here. 43 FPS.").
- **Gameplay Mechanics:**
    - Designing jump sequences and platforming challenges that are fun and not "jank."
    - Considering speedrun strategies and how they impact level design. ("I want to see what the speedrun strat would be like.")
    - Deciding whether to support or fix unintended mechanics like BLJs.
- **Aesthetics and Theming:** Making choices about textures, color palettes, and object design to fit the "space plantation" theme (e.g., designing "low-poly crops," water tanks).
- **Player Guidance:** Using elements like signs ("We also have these signs now that just point to the factory") to improve navigation.
- **Iterative Design:** Constantly tweaking elements based on testing and feedback (e.g., platform placement, texture choices, effect intensity).
