## Game Development Process
- The primary focus of the current development stage is the design and structuring of the game's overworld, which is described as "massive."
- Kaze is working on the "general shape of the overworld" and then plans to "start honing in on the individual areas."
- The hub world is being "completely remaking it basically."
- A significant ongoing task is finalizing the game's story, particularly the motivations for Bowser and the details of the final (third) Bowser level and boss fight.
- The overworld is planned to have 13 main land/underwater parts, plus 2 additional cloud parts, making 15 distinct areas.
- The game will feature 15 main course entrances, 4 cap statues, and at least 5 side levels (with potential for more).
- 21 stars are planned for the overworld itself, comprising secret levels, special events, and secret stars.

## Personal Insights
- Kaze expresses a strong preference for an interconnected overworld where player actions in one area can have effects or unlock content in earlier areas: "What I like about this is how it's connected... I think that's how it should be."
- He values creating a sense of anticipation for the player by allowing them to see landmarks of future, yet inaccessible, areas: "I think the main part about this is that we have landmarks in future parts that you look forward to exploring that's like the most important principle."
- Kaze is enthusiastic about the potential for dynamic events in the overworld, such as a "pirate invasion."
- He is concerned with story coherence, wanting later plot points to build logically on earlier ones, and debates extensively on making the final boss thematically appropriate (e.g., "it doesn't build on what happened before I want to symbol on what happened before").
- Kaze expresses enthusiasm for the current overworld plan: "I'm really enthusiastic about the overworld."

## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Overworld Shape & Structure:** The overworld will be "YoshiShaped," but not perfectly, and so large that its shape might not be apparent during gameplay. It's divided into 15 distinct loading zones/parts.
- **Progression and Unlocks:** Progression is gated by star counts and specific events/actions. For example, beating Bowser 1 unlocks a new section of the overworld. Some areas require specific cap abilities or solving puzzles (e.g., rotating a lighthouse).
- **Story Integration:** A major challenge is crafting a coherent story, particularly Bowser's motivations and the nature of the third Bowser level. The team is exploring themes like Bowser as a "ruthless capitalist."
    - "I feel like to make the third Bowser level we need to make the story first and we haven't really done that."
    - "Our number one concern is what is the third bowser level and number two is what other events we want to add."
- **Final Boss Conceptualization:** Extensive discussion focuses on the final boss encounter, with ideas including a Mecha Bowser level, a Giga Bowser fight (Mario also becoming Giga), and using the Super Happy Tree (or a "Super Sad Tree" variant) as a mechanic. The challenge lies in making this feel like a natural culmination of the story and gameplay.
- **Course Design Gimmicks:** Examples include Course 5 (Wario Zoo) where the ticket price is always one more coin than the player has, requiring an alternative entry method.
- **Interconnectivity:** A core design goal is to have different parts of the overworld influence each other, with later game actions potentially opening up things in earlier areas.
