## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Key Decisions:**
    - Creation of an in-game Mario painting minigame where player drawings are evaluated for a star.
    - Selection of the "Mario Paint Robot" as the NPC to judge the artwork, based partly on community suggestion.
    - Deliberate exclusion of an "undo" feature for the painting mechanic, opting for a "reset" button instead to maintain a sense of consequence.
    - Use of dynamically added virtual textures for the painting surface.
    - Implementing point-filtering for Mario's texture in the painting minigame for a "crisper" look.
- **Challenges Faced & Solutions:**
    - **Fill Algorithm Performance:** Standard recursive fill algorithms would overflow the N64's limited stack. Kaze "had to invent something" â€“ a non-recursive, stack-based pixel fill method.
    - **Bug Fixing:** Addressed bugs like an infinite score exploit by ensuring the score resets correctly, requiring code refactoring.
    - **Asset Creation:** Difficulty in finding a suitable rip of the Mario Paint Robot sprite led to Kaze deciding to remake the character in 3D.
    - **Emulator Compatibility:** Encountered a persistent crash with "Return to Yoshi's Island" on the Parallel RDP plugin for emulators, which remained unresolved despite consultation.
    - **Score Logic:** Fine-tuning the scoring mechanism for the painting minigame to allow some margin of error while still requiring a recognizable Mario.
