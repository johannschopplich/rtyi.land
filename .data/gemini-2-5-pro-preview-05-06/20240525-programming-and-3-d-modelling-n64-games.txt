## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Star 1 Acquisition (Central Challenge):**
    - Goal: Player sees Star 1 early but can't reach it immediately, creating a mystery.
    - Discarded ideas for access: Mirror reflecting the star, complex chandelier puzzle to break an ice wall.
    - Considered ideas: Power-up (Bomb Cap, but rejected making it mandatory for Star 1), cannon.
    - Chosen solution: A large snowball, found on an upper platform, which the player rolls down to smash an ice wall concealing the star. This was deemed thematically appropriate and understandable.
- **Chandelier Mechanic:**
    - Concept: A swingable chandelier for platforming, player rocks it to build momentum.
    - Technical challenge: Programming the swing (quaternions vs. simpler single-axis). Kaze opted for single-axis for easier programming and better player experience.
    - Application: Initially considered for breaking the ice wall for Star 1, but later decided to use it for platforming sections elsewhere and for introducing the mechanic in a dedicated room.
- **Level Flow and Layout:**
    - Designing interconnected paths and sub-areas (e.g., "snowman cave path," "cold haze room").
    - Using visual cues like spiral staircases to guide the player's view and create interesting level geometry.
    - Balancing open-ended exploration with clear objectives.
- **Visuals and Effects:**
    - Inventing a "seamless snowflake pattern" for textures.
    - Improving a "growing path" effect to be less abrupt and more visually appealing.
    - Planning distinct theming for different rooms (e.g., lobby, diner, library) post-platforming design.
- **Player Experience:**
    - Avoiding mandatory power-ups for early stars (e.g., Bomb Cap for Star 1).
    - Ensuring new mechanics are introduced clearly to the player.
    - Striving for "cool" and memorable moments.
- **Enemies/NPCs:** Decision made for "only enemies" and "no NPCs."
- **Yoshi Coin Ideas:** Brainstorming collectible challenges, such as smashing an ice statue with a snowball or finding a non-solid wall.
