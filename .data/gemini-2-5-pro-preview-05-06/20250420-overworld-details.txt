## Game Development Process
- **Audio Engine Rewrite:** Kaze recently spent a week rewriting Mario 64's audio engine, making it more memory-efficient, adding new capabilities, and ensuring he understands the entire codebase for easier debugging. It's now "mostly all my own code," except for the RSP interfacing.
- **Overworld Development:** Current work is focused on the overworld for "Return to Yoshi's Island." Sand and wall textures have been selected. The stream focuses on creating water effects and selecting wood textures.
- **Iterative Feature Implementation:** The development of water effects is shown as a highly iterative process, involving conceptualization, coding, testing in-game, debugging, and visual/parameter refinement.
- **Asset Creation/Selection:** Kaze is actively choosing and implementing textures and materials for the game environment.
- **Performance and Limitations:** Constant awareness of N64 limitations (e.g., triangle counts for water), but also a willingness to push them. The game is targeted for 64MB.

## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Water Effects:** A significant portion of the stream is dedicated to designing and implementing dynamic water waves for a "tropical beach" feel. This involves:
    - **Challenge:** Making waves look good, directional (coming into the island), and interact well with swimming mechanics without looking "weird."
    - **Solutions Explored/Implemented:** Sine waves, considering Wind Waker/Mario Sunshine/Wave Race 64 styles (mostly dismissed for various reasons), hex tessellation, texture scrolling, vertex manipulation for wave motion and height, dynamic vertex coloring for depth, and creating a curvature to the wave front.
    - **Challenge:** Performance cost of complex waves (e.g., Gerstner waves deemed too expensive).
- **Console Compatibility:** "Return to Yoshi's Island" (referred to as "Yoshi Garden version" by a chatter) is "very unlikely" to have full N64 console compatibility. This is due to historical tool limitations and a past lack of focus on console compatibility in the N64 modding scene.
- **Overworld Design:** The game aims for an "open zone" feel for the overworld, without loading zones.
- **Aesthetics vs. Gameplay:** Kaze grapples with balancing visually impressive effects (like high waves) with practical gameplay implications (Mario clipping or swimming awkwardly).
- **Texture Tiling:** Uses noise textures to mitigate obvious tiling patterns on surfaces like wood.
- **Future Water Features:** Plans to add foam near the shore, with opacity linked to wave dynamics.

## Business Aspects
