## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Challenge: Creating a convincing styrofoam material with dynamic lighting on N64.** This is the central focus of the transcript. Kaze wants it to "look as close to real styrofoam as I can," with highlights that appear as the object/camera moves.
    - Initial attempts included looking for existing textures, trying to modify them, considering sea sponge or pebble textures, and even AI generation, none of which were satisfactory.
    - Kaze explored 3D modeling the texture in Blender, using displacement maps, and environment maps, encountering difficulties with tools and achieving the desired effect.
- **Challenge: N64 hardware limitations.**
    - Standard bump mapping is not feasible: "There was this article about bump mapping on n64, but it relies on being a specific way if I remember correctly." and "The N64 also can't do normal maps [natively]."
    - Overdraw is a concern: "Why skate that bump mapping but it has four times over draw and it only works on one normal song."
- **Solution/Decision: Implementing a fake bump mapping/dynamic lighting effect using normal maps and CPU pre-computation.**
    - The plan involves using a base texture and a separate highlight texture (or deriving highlights from a normal map).
    - The highlights' visibility will be controlled by vertex alpha, calculated based on the angle between the surface, the light source, and the camera.
    - To make this feasible for various surface orientations, Kaze decides: "What if the styrofoam was only allowed to be at like four or five different rotations... I'm thinking we're going to limit ourselves to six rotations. Or maybe eight." This involves pre-calculating or having multiple versions of the normal map for different orientations.
    - "We have to compute the output of the normal map before we pass this to the renderer on the CPU, which fortunately is cheap, which is why we used 16 directions [in the palette for grayscale values]."
- **Challenge: Texture tiling and appearance.** Kaze notes issues like "how obviously this texture repeats" and textures having "lighting baked in" when he needs a neutral base.
