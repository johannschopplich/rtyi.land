## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Sound Engine Overhaul:** A major decision was to rip out and remake the Mario 64 sound engine due to its perceived flaws, aiming for better performance, smaller size, and easier game development.
- **Overworld Design & Cohesion:** Challenges include logically placing key elements (e.g., "Boshi's platform is here, but then Bill's drill is here. And Boshi being right next to the drill is all awkward"), visually representing concepts like a magical barrier around Bowser's drill, and filling large empty areas with meaningful content like a Yoshi playground or hospital.
- **Scope Management for Side Levels:** Kaze actively decides against overly complex new side levels: "The issue I have with it is it's too much work... I want the side levels that we now add to the project to be side levels that are based on a mechanic we already have." This led to the concept of "single mechanic levels."
- **Level Flow and Player Guidance:** Identified issues with the "Grins and Badland" level, where players struggle to find objectives, requiring a redesign. "You enter the level and you kind of have no fucking idea what to do."
- **Implementing New Ideas:** Brainstorming and problem-solving for new level concepts, such as how to make a hide-and-seek level engaging or how an egg-rolling/pinball mechanic would work and fit thematically.
- **Technical Implementation Details:** Briefly touches on issues like getting a temporary skybox to render correctly ("Why is RenderInfo.bg missing now?").
