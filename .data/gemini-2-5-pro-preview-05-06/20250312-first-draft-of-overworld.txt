## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Overworld Structure & Navigation:**
    - The overworld is a "gigantic" island in the "shape of a Yoshi hat." A major challenge is its current size ("the overload is way too big") and making it feel engaging rather than empty.
    - Progression between overworld sections is a key focus, with discussions on using the ice cap to reach new areas, and designing transitions (e.g., caves, fog) to make sense of distinct zones. ("First of all, you need the ice cap to get to the next part...").
    - Preventing players from sequence-breaking or getting lost: "How do we prevent the player from just forever walking around the beach?"
- **Course Entry Mechanics:**
    - **Zoo (Course 4):** Extensive discussion on how the player will enter. Ideas include Wario charging coins, a ticket system (monkey trading a ticket for a watermelon, or a penguin providing a ticket after being "caught"). The goal is to make it an engaging puzzle/quest. ("The way you enter this is that you actually buy a ticket... A monkey somewhere else tells you that he will trade you a zoo ticket for a watermelon.")
    - **Initial Course (Bunny Brushwoods):** Player needs 10 coins to get Pachyderm to move, or can ground pound him temporarily. This forces initial exploration.
- **Ice Cap Mechanics:**
    - The ice cap is a crucial item for progression ("you need the ice cap to get to the next part").
    - Challenges include implementing its functionality correctly (debugging missing models/behaviors) and designing puzzles around its use (e.g., reaching higher areas, walking on water/waterfalls).
- **Player Experience and Guidance:**
    - Balancing difficulty and intuition for puzzles and progression.
    - Tutorializing new mechanics like swimming: "there's going to be a cheep cheep coming up to you with a sign that has just a quick overview of the water controls."
    - Avoiding "false positive feedback" and ensuring design choices are genuinely good, not just liked by fans.
- **Technical Limitations:**
    - Polygon count concerns for rendering distant areas like the zoo from the main overworld.
    - Dealing with engine quirks or bugs ("Why, why the f**k is he swimming? Oh I know why. I hate this.").
