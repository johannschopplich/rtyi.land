## Game Development Process
- The team is actively working on boss fight mechanics, specifically implementing a Bowser boss fight where players must grab his tail and throw him off a ship
- The final game is "so unrecognizable from the demo right now it's actually crazy" - indicating significant evolution and improvements since the public demo
- Development appears to be in an advanced stage with polish work being done on animations, collision detection, and visual effects
- The ROM is currently 44.6 megabytes, with 32.1 megabytes being music (using streamed music for better quality despite larger file size)
- Kaze estimates "at least another year before it comes out"
- The project transitioned from being Kaze's full-time focus to a side project when he got a job about half a year ago

## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- Implementing a unique Bowser fight where players grab his tail and throw him off a ship, with the difficulty increasing each time
- Adding ranking systems similar to Mario Galaxy's green stars, with special "Yoshi stars" that can be collected
- Dealing with technical challenges including collision detection bugs, animation interpolation issues at different frame rates
- Decision to use streamed music for better quality despite larger file size constraints
- Movement improvements allowing long jumps out of long jump landings and jumps out of triple jump landings for better game feel
- Implementing visual polish like dust effects when Bowser slides and spawn-in animations for background ships

## Business Aspects

- Plans to delay open-sourcing: "we should probably delay open sourcing the game a little from the initial release, just so Nintendo doesn't instantly take it down... Maybe like two months"
