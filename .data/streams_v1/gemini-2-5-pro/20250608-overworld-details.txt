## Game Development Process
- **Project Focus:** The stream is entirely dedicated to the development of "Return to Yoshi's Island," described as a "huge game."
- **Current Stage:** Kaze is working on various levels and systems. Specific tasks mentioned include finalizing "Course 7" by adding Yoshi coins, 3D modeling for an overworld area, and planning the integration of a new Luigi character model.
- **Development Pipeline:** The process involves 3D modeling and UV mapping in Blender, texture creation/editing in image software (GIMP), importing assets into the game, and testing performance directly on N64-like emulators and hardware.
- **Upcoming Milestones:**
    - Adding five Yoshi coins to "Career 7" (Carnival level).
    - Importing a new Luigi model, which requires cleaning up unused files to free up RAM.
    - Modeling future overworld areas, including a beach, Bowser's oil tanker, and a forest.
- **Technical Foundation:** The game is built on the Mario 64 decompilation project, which allows for deeper modifications than traditional ROM hacking. The entire project is being developed with real N64 hardware as the target platform.

## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Technical Limitations as a Design Constraint:** A core challenge is working within the N64's limitations. This is a point of pride, with Kaze aiming for high visual quality while maintaining performance on real hardware. He mentions framerate drops and memory constraints (needing to delete files to fit Luigi in RAM).
- **Innovative Texturing Method:** Kaze explains a technique to achieve higher-than-native texture resolution: "we can have multiple textures on them, which gives us a fake texture resolution that's way higher than what the N64 would normally be able to do."
- **Asset Creation Workflow Issues:** A significant portion of the stream is dedicated to the challenge of finding or creating a suitable mushroom floor texture. This involves failed attempts with AI texture generators and struggles with new image editing software (Krita vs. GIMP) to correctly format the texture (seamless, 16-color indexed with correct alpha).
- **Character Addition:** A key design decision is the plan to add Luigi as a playable character, using a new model from Pixelpalabra. This is presented as a significant upcoming feature.
