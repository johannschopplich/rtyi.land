## Game Development Process
- **Audio Optimization:** Significant progress in refactoring audio code, achieving at least a 12.5% speed-up, with further improvements ongoing.
- **Overworld Development:** Actively planning overworld events and stars. The current goal is 180 total stars, with 21 in the overworld (11 planned, 10 more needed during this stream). The overworld is considered a critical and large level requiring perfection.
- **Content Creation:** Brainstorming and designing numerous secret stars and NPC interactions for the overworld, aiming to make it feel "alive." This includes ideas like NPCs from levels visiting the overworld, Yoshi-specific stars (house rebuilding, harvesting, egg hatching), and unique character appearances (Goomba King, Mole Blacksmith).
- **Beta Testing:** Regularly conducting beta tests for individual levels (e.g., Course 3, Course 11) by sending ROMs to testers. This process is yielding many minor improvements and validating design choices.
- **Level Integration:** Integrating contributor levels (e.g., AzaEasy's Course 11) into the overworld structure.
- **Technical Exploration:** Investigating new animation formats (referred to as "Cauterinian animations") to support features like squash and stretch, which are not natively supported by the Mario 64 engine.
- **Iterative Design:** Employing a drafting process for the hub world to manage layout, compactness, and player pathing, allowing for easy adjustments.

## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Overworld Interactivity:** A major focus is making the overworld dynamic, with NPCs from various courses appearing and offering star opportunities.
- **Star Design:** Extensive brainstorming for unique and engaging star concepts, such as the Yoshi egg hatching over real game time, a harvesting minigame, or the Goomba King fishing.
- **NPC Purpose:** Insistence that any NPC appearing in the overworld must have a functional role, preferably tied to a star.
- **Hub World Layout:** Addressing the challenge of making the hub world compact and minimizing "unnecessary walking" through iterative drafting.
- **Engine Limitations (Animation):** The SM64 engine's lack of native support for squash and stretch is a recognized challenge, with potential new animation formats being considered as a solution.
- **Visual Cohesion and "Juiciness":** Striving for a vibrant and appealing visual style, discussing how to improve less "juicy" areas, and considering new graphics combiners.
- **Connectivity:** Designing elements to create a sense of a connected world, e.g., a cave near Rainbow Grotto offering a view into the level or containing thematic elements like crystals.
- **Player Guidance/Secrets:** Designing secret stars in the overworld cave that might involve carrying a light source or navigating in the dark, balancing challenge with discoverability.
