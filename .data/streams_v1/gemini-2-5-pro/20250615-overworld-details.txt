## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Technical Challenge: N64 Memory Limits:**
    - The most dramatic challenge in the stream was a crash when loading the new "Dino Dino Domain."
    - After extensive debugging, the root cause was discovered: the new, more detailed level caused the game to exceed its available RAM by a mere 1 kilobyte.
    - The solution was to remove an unused asset (a "leaf boat") from the game's shared resources, freeing up just enough space for the level to load. This exemplifies the fight against the N64's strict hardware constraints.
- **Technical Challenge: Frame-Perfect Bugs:**
    - The stream begins with Kaze debugging a "nasty bug" where pressing 'A' one frame before landing crashes the game. He eventually traces the issue to how the game handles animation changes in a single frame, showcasing the difficulty of fixing subtle, timing-based issues.
- **Creative Design: Enhancing Player Impact:**
    - A significant design idea was brainstormed live on stream for the nightclub area. Instead of a simple star pickup, Kaze and his chat developed a sequence where the player grabs a pole, causing the disco ball (containing the star) to fall and shatter. This action would then cause the room to go dark, the music to stop, and the dancing Shy Guys to halt, creating a real sense of "impact of your actions."
- **Player Onboarding and Tutorials:**
    - Kaze realizes that the game currently lacks a proper tutorial for the wall kick, a crucial mechanic. He discusses the need to either reinstate a removed tutorial from an earlier course or find other ways to teach the player, showing a conscious effort to consider the experience of new players.
