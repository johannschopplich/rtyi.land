## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
-   **Aesthetic Cohesion:** Maintaining a consistent and believable "craft world" look across diverse materials (styrofoam, paper, felt, crayons, cardboard) is a primary goal and ongoing challenge.
-   **Technical Limitations of N64:**
    -   **Normal Maps:** The N64 lacks native normal map support. Kaze implemented a custom solution where textures are computed on the CPU for each orientation to simulate dynamic lighting/reflections on surfaces like styrofoam. This is performance-intensive.
    -   **Rendering Costs:** Many detailed craft ideas (e.g., pipe cleaners, frills on umbrellas) are "very Expensive to render realistically" on the N64, forcing design compromises or simpler approaches.
    -   **Emulator Compatibility:** Using newer F3DX features could break emulator compatibility, which is a concern ("Emulators are holding us back like that").
-   **Level and Biome Design:**
    -   The "icy section" is proving difficult to conceptualize with craft materials ("Maybe ice just doesn't work"). Kaze is considering changing it to a castle biome or a paper mache volcano.
    -   Designing engaging platforming sequences (e.g., the rolling crayon, paper plane flight, falling cardboard bridges).
-   **Enemy Design:**
    -   Brainstorming enemies that fit the craft aesthetic (e.g., scissors, cardboard Thwomps/Bombs/Whomps, whiteboard eraser, cardboard saw blades).
    -   Avoiding designs that might be too similar to other games like "Paper Mario the Origami King."
-   **Lighting and Textures:**
    -   Significant effort is put into making lighting and textures convey the properties of materials (e.g., styrofoam's reflectivity, felt's dullness).
    -   Kaze frequently searches for and edits textures to fit the aesthetic and technical constraints.
-   **Player Experience:** Ensuring quests are clear (e.g., Luigi's crayon) and platforming challenges are fair but engaging.
