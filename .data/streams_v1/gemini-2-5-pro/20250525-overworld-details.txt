## Game Development Process
- **Overall Progress:** The overworld is estimated to be around 30% complete. Kaze has finished the "first pass" for the first four overworld areas out of a total of fourteen.
- **Current Focus:** The stream focuses on level design and art for a specific cave area in the overworld, trying to finalize its layout, solve player frustration points, and implement a forgotten star concept.
- **Development Stages:** The project is in a state where beta testing is beginning. Kaze mentions the game is "almost in a state where we would be able to just release the game," but there is still a significant amount of polishing he wants to do, particularly on the overworld.
- **Dependencies:** Development is occasionally stalled by external factors, such as waiting for a bug fix in the `fast64` toolchain. Kaze will only look into it himself if a fix doesn't materialize after a long time.

## Personal Insights
- **Design Philosophy:** Kaze believes it's much easier to make a fun Mario hack than a fun Zelda hack. He posits that Mario's core mechanics are inherently enjoyable, allowing for simple "playground" level design, whereas Zelda requires much more intricate design work (puzzles, story) to be engaging.
- **Creative Process:** He is very open about the challenges of development, such as forgetting key design ideas and having to rediscover them by reviewing old footage or relying on his community's memory. He also describes the "harsh wake up call" every game designer faces when playtesting reveals their game "actually sucks."
- **On Content Creation:** Kaze is pragmatic about his YouTube channel, stating he creates more videos because he needs to "feed my family." He admits the popular video series was a direct result of a Squarespace sponsorship and would not have existed otherwise.
- **Attitude:** He describes his persona as "realistic, not humble," and is comfortable stating his own past achievements (e.g., being "cracked at Melee") or praising his own work (e.g., Missing Link).

## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Challenge: Player Frustration:** Kaze identified that falling into the water in a specific cave area was an overly harsh punishment ("the worst punishment the gods can bestow upon you").
- **Solution:** He decided to add a series of small, challenging platforms to allow a skilled player to get back to the start without a full reset, making the penalty less severe.
- **Challenge: Forgotten Idea:** He forgot the specific star mission for the cave. A viewer ("gargoyle") reminded him of the idea: the player must carry a crystal, which reveals a temporary path around them.
- **Iterative Design:** Kaze refined the crystal idea on stream, considering making the path *always* appear dimly around Mario but become more visible while holding the crystal.
- **Innovative Feature:** The game features a "one-way mirror" effect where players in the overworld can look through a portal and see into a playable course, making the world feel more connected.

## Business Aspects
- **Beta Testing:** Kaze is recruiting beta testers via a Google Form shared with his stream viewers. He is deliberately not using Patreon for recruitment to avoid potential "legal trouble." The focus is on "gameplay testing," not explicit bug hunting.
- **Sponsorships:** He is very transparent about his sponsorship model. He works with Squarespace on a flat-rate fee of "$2500 flat per video." He explicitly states he would never take a conversion-based deal, as it shifts all the business risk onto the creator.
- **Funding:** YouTube content, funded by sponsorships like Squarespace, is a primary income source that supports his family and, by extension, his ability to work on game development projects.
