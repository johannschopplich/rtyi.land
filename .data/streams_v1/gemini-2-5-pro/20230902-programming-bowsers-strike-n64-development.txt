## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Visual Fidelity vs. Performance:** A recurring theme, e.g., choosing RGBA16 over CI4 for smoother texture edges despite larger size, or optimizing fish models to use fewer textures for batch rendering.
- **Lighting and Shading:** Extensive discussion on how to light models â€“ baked lighting in textures vs. dynamic level lighting vs. vertex colors. Kaze generally prefers models to interact with world/level lighting ("it's usually better to have it work with world lights so that it actually looks like it's placed in the world"). This often involves complex combiner setups and texture adjustments.
- **Tool-Induced Challenges (Fast64):** Many problems are attributed to Fast64 bugs or limitations:
    - Armature import issues ("if you reimport the armature, Fuzz64 will fuck it up").
    - Texture loading order affecting performance if not handled correctly (Goomba feet texture).
    - Rendering inconsistencies between editor and game.
- **Floating Point Exceptions on N64:** Identified as an "annoying as fuck" issue to fix for several levels when running on actual N64 hardware.
- **Animation Design for Interactive Objects:**
    - **Cap Switches:** Kaze opts for using multiple distinct models for different states of cap switches rather than "switch bones" due to perceived unreliability and difficulty of the latter.
    - **Blue Coin Switch:** Decides against a complex animation due to its brief on-screen time (5 frames).
- **Gameplay Feel and Object Interaction:** Considers how objects behave and feel, e.g., the impact sound for a switch ("Okay, I want the stone sound"), how Mario interacts with new collision, and the visual feedback of a switch being pressed.
- **Reimagining Vanilla Elements:** Conscious decision to replace or significantly alter original SM64 models and behaviors Kaze deems subpar (e.g., "All the vanilla models are awful").
