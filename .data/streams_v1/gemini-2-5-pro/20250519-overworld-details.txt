## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
-   **Fast Travel System:** A major design decision was made to implement fast travel via a map in the pause menu. This completely shifted the design focus for the overworld away from managing travel time to instead ensuring there are no "vast empty stretches of land."
-   **Accessibility:** The game will include options that allow "even bad players can play the game," which Kaze acknowledges is akin to allowing cheating. This shows a clear design choice to prioritize accessibility and a wider audience.
-   **Feature vs. Feel:** Kaze weighs the addition of new features against their impact on gameplay. He considers adding rope physics (which are already coded) but is "a bit worried that it's going to feel jank to play with," demonstrating a trade-off between visual dynamism and solid platforming feel.
-   **Technical Problem-Solving:** A recurring challenge is dealing with the quirks of the N64 engine and the modding toolchain. Kaze mentions re-importing assets to fix bugs and his ongoing efforts to understand and optimize for N64-specific features like texture filtering and resolution modes.
-   **Overworld Pacing:** Kaze actively refines the overworld layout to improve pacing, shortening a long, straight path and changing a corridor's shape because "it looks much better being so short."

## Business Aspects
-   **Commercial Awareness:** While this project is a non-commercial ROM hack, Kaze shows awareness of commercial realities, stating if he had to develop for another console, he'd "probably pick like the Switch 2 because I could actually make commercial games with that that realistically someone would buy."
-   **Distribution:** He confirms that the final game "can exist on a real cartridge," addressing a key question about how the game can be played by the community.
