- The game "Return to Yoshi's Island" (RTYI) is stated to be "getting close to completion."
- Current work involves developing "course 15," which includes implementing a "zipper" feature leading to a lava area and a platforming section.
- Significant time is spent on bug fixing, particularly for a "snow bug" and various crashes, many of which are emulator-specific.
- Kaze is actively working on optimizations. He mentions a "cool optimization" for the snow effect and notes that since a previous "Source Code video," he has made the game "like twice as fast again."
- The development process involves iterative design, such as deciding on the placement of features ("I think we said we want the zipper to be around here") and addressing visual elements like the styrofoam texture.

## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Aesthetic Choices:**
    - The craft-based theme is prominent (cardboard, paper, staples, "mushrooms here that are just an inverted plate with a toilet roll").
    - Kaze debates the "normal map styrofoam" effect, questioning its appearance and performance, ultimately deciding to "replace styrofoam with cardboard and paper staple."
    - Discussion on how to represent the "death void" below levels, considering options like a table, white void, or a gradient blue void suggested by Biobak.
- **Level Design (Course 15):**
    - Planning a "zipper to hell" (a lava area).
    - Designing platforming sections, including tilting platforms, and tuning their difficulty ("Okay, now I made it too easy").
    - Considering player guidance and preventing them from jumping to their death ("I also really want to prevent the player thinking that they can jump down and see something down there").
- **Technical Challenges & Solutions:**
    - **Emulator Issues:** A major recurring challenge is dealing with emulator-specific crashes and behaviors that don't occur on real N64 hardware or other emulators. This consumes significant debugging time ("The worst problem is that on real n64 it legit just works"). Kaze mentions specific issues with Project64 and Parallel Launcher.
    - **Performance Optimization:** Constant effort to maintain good frame rates (aiming for 30 FPS) on N64 hardware. This involves optimizing code, effects (e.g., snow), and level geometry. He discusses the performance cost of anti-aliasing and mipmapping.
    - **Memory Management/Corruption:** Investigating crashes potentially caused by memory allocation issues ("Surely this isn't too big an allocation," "What if this caused some kind of memory corruption somehow?").
    - **Visual Effects:** Implementing and debugging complex visual effects like snow particles and normal mapping for grass/styrofoam, ensuring they work correctly and perform well.
- **Gameplay Mechanics:**
    - Discussion of "vanilla behavior" from Mario 64 and whether to retain or change it (e.g., camera behavior when hitting 'C' while running).
    - Adjusting the sensitivity of tilting platforms.
