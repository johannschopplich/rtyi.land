## Game Development Process
- The game is stated to be approximately 75% complete ("75 mean Quick 64. Yeah").
- Current focus includes integrating character assets (models, animations like Wiggler's) and programming their behavior.
- Development of specific levels and quests is ongoing, such as "Bowser's office," the "Union Mission" involving a strike and a "strike breaker" boss, and the "Wild West Valley" level.

## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Level Reworks:** The "Biobak" level was significantly changed to feel more "vast" and "near infinite" with an improved cloud ocean and lighting.
- **Camera System:** Dealing with camera issues in tight spaces (like the elevator) is a challenge. Planned solutions include sphere collision for the camera and potentially fixed camera angles in specific locations.
- **Hitbox Design:** Approximating complex character shapes (like Wiggler) with N64's limited cylindrical hitboxes requires creative solutions ("What if he's just two cylinders like this?").
- **Quest/Narrative Design:** The "Union Mission" features a multi-stage narrative where minions strike, Bowser sends a "strike breaker," the plaza becomes ominous, and a boss (oil monster) attacks Mario.
- **Debugging:** A significant portion of the stream shows Kaze actively debugging various issues, such as elevator mechanics, object positioning (Toxboxes), and texture/animation glitches (Wiggler's face with Fast64).
- **Wiggler's Interactive Dialogue:** Wiggler's dialogue will change based on which parts of the construction site Mario has interfered with, serving as a hint system.
- **Technical Limitations:** Constant need to work within the N64's constraints regarding performance, rendering, and object complexity.

## Business Aspects
- **Release Strategy:** Kaze prefers releasing games around September, believing it's a time when Nintendo is less likely to issue takedowns.
- **Monetization/Distribution:**
    - He will not sell physical cartridges due to legal risks ("if I do I will get sued to shit").
    - He might sell merchandise like game boxes and instruction booklets, which could include a QR code for downloading the ROM (not from him directly).
- **Marketing:**
    - Uses YouTube Shorts for promotion, paying an editor ($5 per short).
    - Analyzes the cost-effectiveness of Shorts, noting they are not consistently profitable but hopes for viral hits.
- **Personal Finances:** Mentions personal financial situation (low income affecting house loan eligibility) which could indirectly influence his work setup.
