## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **New Mario Model Implementation:**
    - **Decision:** To adopt and integrate Biobak's new, significantly improved Mario model.
    - **Challenges:** Ensuring anti-aliasing on the cap's 'M' logo, managing dynamic color changes for different caps (requiring palette shifting), fixing vertex normal issues causing "eyeshadow," optimizing rendering for N64 combiners, and achieving desired lighting effects on buttons (shiny vs. glowing). Kaze ultimately decides to use an I4 texture for the hat logo to solve anti-aliasing issues, accepting the trade-off of needing to dynamically change all 16 palette colors for cap changes.
- **Light Bridge Mechanic:**
    - **Decision:** To be player-triggered via an "arcade button," with clear visual warnings before it disappears.
    - **Challenge:** Implementing transparency and effective visual feedback (e.g., gradients, dithering) within N64 capabilities.
- **Aesthetic Details:**
    - **Decision/Debate:** The "butt crease" on Mario's model was a point of discussion, with Kaze deciding to add/enhance it despite Biobak potentially not wanting it initially.
- **N64 Hardware Limitations:**
    - **Challenge:** Constantly working within and around the N64's limitations regarding TMEM, texture formats (CI8, I4, RGBA16), rendering cycles, and combiner logic.
    - **Challenge:** Debugging issues that could stem from Fast64 (the SM64 decompilation project base), emulator inaccuracies, or actual N64 hardware quirks, particularly with alpha blending and lighting.
- **Color Perception Anomaly:**
    - **Challenge:** Kaze perceived a crystal in-game as green, while its RGB values and others' perception indicated yellow/orange. He suspected it might be due to an overload of light sources affecting Mario.
