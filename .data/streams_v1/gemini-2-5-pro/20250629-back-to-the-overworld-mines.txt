## Game Development Process
- The project is divided into major sections, with "part 1" described as "slowly getting done."
- Kaze is currently focused on implementing features in the game's overworld, such as programming doors and adding interactive NPCs.
- Development involves a cycle of coding new features, live testing, polishing visual effects, and then fixing bugs that arise. A major blocker identified during the stream is a game-breaking bug in the dialogue system, a result of a recent code refactor, which needs to be fixed before NPC work can continue.
- Progress is iterative. A level was recently finished off-stream and showcased on a real N64. Kaze is also planning future content, such as a "sand form" for Mario.
- Optimization is a continuous process. Kaze mentions plans to convert 4x4 matrices to 4x3 matrices for a "pretty big optimization" and has already implemented custom solutions to improve performance, like replacing standard behavior loops.

## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Cutscene Design:** A major creative decision was made to redesign the game's intro. The new concept involves a transition from a 3D scene to a 2D "storybook" style (inspired by *Yoshi's Story*), which then fades back into the 3D world to begin gameplay. This idea was brainstormed live on stream.
- **Game Economy:** Kaze is implementing a coin-based economy to gate progress. He designed an NPC ("Corrupt Dom") who requires 200 coins to grant the player access to a cannon, stating, "it's important to me that some things just cost money."
- **Visual Polish:** Significant time is spent refining particle effects. Kaze iterates on a new star unlock animation, adding a "pixie dust" shower effect that he decides is much better than the standard animation and should be used for all stars.
- **Technical Challenge (Dialogue System):** A critical bug was discovered where the entire dialogue system is broken, returning a null value and preventing interaction with NPCs. This halted the implementation of the new cannon NPC and was deemed too significant to fix on stream.
- **Resource Management:** Kaze performs a calculation of the potential memory footprint of the new storybook cutscene, concluding it might take half a megabyte, which would need to be streamed from memory. He assesses the total remaining ROM space (estimating 5-7 MB) and determines the idea is feasible.

## Business Aspects
- **Distribution Model:** The game is not currently open source, but Kaze states, "It will be open source though, once it's done." He differentiates between the game and the underlying engine, parts of which are already available.
- **Potential Funding/Asset Creation:** When contemplating a copyright-free version of the game, Kaze mentions the future possibility of paying artists for original textures. He notes, "I have to pay a lot of artists to make textures for us. And then you get to dictate to these artists what we want."
