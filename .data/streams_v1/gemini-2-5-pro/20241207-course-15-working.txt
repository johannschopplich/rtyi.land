## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Player Guidance:** A major focus. Beta testing for "Course 3" revealed significant issues with players getting lost or missing entire sections. Kaze plans to make initial objectives (like Star 1) much more obvious, drawing inspiration from Mario 64's level design.
    - Example: "The person just didn't notice that there was a whole level here. So that's going to be interesting to figure out how to make it more likely that people realize there's a level."
- **Level Theming and Mechanics:** Astro Arcade is a "space arcade" with pinball mechanics. Kaze is developing various interactive elements like flippers, bumpers, and trampolines.
- **Aesthetic Design:** Significant effort is put into textures, lighting, and overall visual appeal. Kaze struggles with certain elements (e.g., "metal pieces" for Astro Arcade initially looking "bad") and seeks fitting textures (e.g., arcade carpet).
- **Technical Challenges:**
    - A "Fast64 bug" related to `ss geotranslate rotate nodes` was preventing correct level export.
    - Managing N64 hardware limitations, including memory and emulator compatibility (e.g., "This game does use the expansion pack... Technically you could make this game without the expansion pack... Although you might lose emulator compatibility.").
- **Difficulty Balancing:** Ensuring levels are challenging but fair. Feedback from testers on difficult jumps in Course 3 and the accessibility of the "bit pipe."
- **Avoiding "Sonic" Comparisons:** Kaze is actively trying to prevent the "Astro Arcade" level from feeling too much like Sonic, rejecting a "ball tube" idea for this reason.
- **Iterative Design:** Kaze is willing to scrap or significantly alter ideas that don't work (e.g., the initial metal piece design). He adds elements like a "cool ring" without a definite function yet, planning to "figure out the use for it later."
