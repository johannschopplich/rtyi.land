## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Core Challenge: Tooling Limitations:** A significant portion of the stream was dedicated to a technical challenge with the Fast64 tool. Kaze needs to dynamically change material properties at runtime without it affecting other parts of the model. The long debate with collaborator "Lila" explores implementing a solution using display list jumps and segmented memory.
- **Design Principle: Constraining the Overworld:** Kaze explicitly states a core design rule is to limit player abilities in the overworld to prevent sequence-breaking and maintain the "illusions" used to create a seamless world. He rejected a suggestion because "It gives you too much freedom and it breaks a lot of the illusions we use in the overworld."
- **Gameplay Mechanic: Interactive Fruit:** The fruit beach area is being designed as an interactive playground. Kaze plans a timed red coin challenge that requires platforming on fruits with different physical properties: "Grapes would be bouncy," while a honeydew melon would be a rolling platform "that kind of works like Mario Galaxy's rolling ball."
- **Lore and Justification:** A key design decision was to create a lore reason for the giant fruit. They decided the fruit grows on the "super happy tree" and Yoshis transport it to the beach, which "also gives us the opportunity to evolve the overworld over time again."
