## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Overworld Progression Gating:** A significant challenge is designing how the player accesses later parts of the overworld (specifically "Part 5," the second main overworld area).
    - Initial idea: A Yoshi repairs a bridge for 100 coins after being freed. Kaze dislikes this because Yoshis aren't known for repairing bridges, and it feels wrong to charge the player after a rescue.
    - Evolved idea: A Dory is found chained in a lake. After the first Bowser is defeated and Yoshis are freed, a Yoshi is found on Dory's back, having freed her. The player can then ride Dory to the new area. This makes Yoshis active helpers.
- **Course 3 Entrance:** Designing the entrance to Course 3. An initial idea involving smashing rocks if Mario has 5 stars was dismissed as the area already requires 5 stars. The current idea is a mouse/mole NPC in a mineshaft who will continue mining (revealing the entrance) for 100 coins. This sets a precedent for coins having utility in the overworld.
- **Player Experience and Difficulty:** Kaze wants to avoid player frustration. For instance, in an area called "New World," he doesn't want the player to easily fall to their doom, as it's not meant to feel overly dangerous. He struggles with finding solutions (e.g., water might not fit the "dry land" theme of an island's middle).
- **Camera Control:** Kaze aims to give players full camera freedom but also wants scenes to look "cool," requiring level design that naturally facilitates good views.
- **Island Shape:** An initial idea to make the main island Yoshi-shaped was deemed not critical and ultimately not closely followed ("This island isn't Yoshi-shaped at all. It's not even close to Yoshi-shaped... The island doesn't need to be shaped like a Yoshi.").
- **Preventing Sequence Breaking ("Cheese"):** While Kaze is aware players will try to find unintended skips, he's generally lenient ("If you find a way around it, be my guest. I don't want to put fake barriers there.").
- **Invisible Walls:** Kaze's engine has reworked collision, so he generally doesn't use invisible walls unless there's "legitimately no level boundary."
