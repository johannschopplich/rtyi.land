## Game Development Process
- A second demo for "Return to Yoshi's Island" was released on the day of the stream. This demo features only "course one" (the first level).
- Kaze plans to upload a full playthrough of the demo the following day.
- The specific level being worked on during the stream has undergone significant changes since previously shown and currently has no name.
- The level's theme is "retrofuturism," envisioned as a space colony from a 1960s perspective.
- Development includes iterating on level layout (e.g., making a gap smaller, designing a climbable rocket, incorporating monorails) and boss mechanics.
- Technical troubleshooting is ongoing, such as an issue where the game doesn't boot on an EverDrive X7, leading to experiments like invalidating RAM before boot.

## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Level Theme & Aesthetics:** The current level is themed around "retrofuturism," specifically a 1960s vision of a space colony, with elements like monorails and a distinct visual style. There's a desire to avoid overly rusty appearances as "things shouldn't rust" in space, despite Kaze initially liking a rusty look.
- **Boss Design (Iterative Process):**
    - Initial idea: "The Blob," a gelatinous mass with a brain, fitting the retro sci-fi theme.
    - Second idea (from viewer suggestion): "Sluggy the Unshaven" from Yoshi's Island.
    - Third idea (developed during stream): A squid-like boss attacked via rifts/black holes, with tentacles. The player would damage it by hitting its eyes when it pokes its head out after shooting lasers. Dynamic lighting from rifts would signal attack origins.
- **Movement Mechanics:**
    - The "Z plus A move" was removed because it was "so strong that like that basically wasn't a game anymore."
    - Spin and ground pound dive attacks were restricted ("you can only spin or ground pound dive at any time") because unrestricted movement was "too broken." Momentum is removed from a spin move to make it a "safety move."
- **Platforming Elements:**
    - Designing a climbable rocket involves figuring out platforming paths and potentially incorporating monorails around it.
    - An idea for a "trampoline" evolved from a jarring bouncy platform to a "shockwave that shoots you upwards whenever you land on it," possibly themed as a speaker.
- **Technical Challenges & Solutions:**
    - The game not booting on EverDrive X7 is a significant issue Kaze is actively trying to solve.
    - Balancing visual detail with N64 performance limitations is a constant consideration ("Speed gains are mostly spent on like more polygons and details... we need the performance gains here").
    - An attempt to improve performance by running code from the cartridge failed.
- **Player Guidance:** For the squid boss, Kaze considers how to make the weak point obvious, suggesting red pulsating eyes after a laser attack. Using dynamic lights from rifts to indicate attack direction is also a way to guide the player.
