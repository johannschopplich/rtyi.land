## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
-   **Key Design Decisions:**
    -   The level is an "ice castle" with an "eerie" and "lonely" vibe, intended as a late-game, more hardcore platforming experience.
    -   Stars planned include red coins outside/in a courtyard, a platforming challenge to a star seen early but reached later (inspired by Big Boo's Haunt), and a boss fight with a Raven.
    -   Specific room concepts include "cold haze rooms" (damaging cold with fire safe spots), a U-shaped or H-shaped castle layout with a bridge, rooms with ice wave generators, spinning ice platforms, appearing paths (Mario Galaxy style), and cracking ice blocks.
    -   Exploration of advanced N64 graphical techniques, such as CPU-generated normal maps for ice reflections and dynamic texture scrolling based on camera angle for shine effects.
    -   Color palette: dark blue, light blue, and white.
    -   Enemies: Ice block enemies (Nappy Net inspired), "ice demons" (made up by Kaze), big snowman enemy (rolls head), ravens (from Yoshi's Island).
-   **Challenges & Solutions:**
    -   **Texture Quantization:** A major hurdle is the N64's limited color depth (5-bits per RGB channel) and 1-bit alpha for RGBA16 textures, which can make detailed textures look "like ass." Kaze experiments with different texture formats (e.g., IA8 for 4-bit alpha) and considers a Blender plugin to preview quantization.
    -   **Achieving Realistic Ice Effects:** Experimenting with CPU-generated normal maps and reflection maps to make ice surfaces look genuinely reflective and deep, despite N64 limitations. This is an expensive technique, limiting the number of surfaces it can be applied to.
    -   **Level Layout:** Designing a "confusing, but not frustrating" labyrinthine castle structure.
    -   **Performance:** Balancing visual fidelity and complex mechanics with N64 performance. The new OS is partly aimed at addressing this.
