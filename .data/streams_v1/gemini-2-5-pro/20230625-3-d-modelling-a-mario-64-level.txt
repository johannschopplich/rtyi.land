## Game Design Decisions/Challenges
- **Feature Changes:** Player skins were initially planned but have been scrapped in favor of allowing players to buy items for "Mario's house."
- **In-Game Economy:** Coins will be used to purchase items, possibly including "mini games" or a Power Star to make difficult stars easier.
- **Level Interactivity:** Focus on unique mechanics like the cement pit with persistent footprints, requiring significant technical problem-solving (decal limits, rendering to texture, footprint rotation).
- **NPC Design:** Detailed brainstorming for construction-themed NPCs (Koopas with builder hats, Thwomps with vests, a Wiggler as an overseer that changes emotions).
- **Technical Limitations (N64):** Recurring challenge. Decisions like making footprints circular instead of rotated are driven by the difficulty of implementing complex graphics operations on N64 hardware. Memory limits for decals and texture resolution are constant considerations.
- **Visual Design:** Significant effort is put into level aesthetics, with Kaze and Biobak iterating on colors and textures to avoid "ugly" levels.
- **Difficulty Balancing:** Kaze adjusts game difficulty based on playtesting, e.g., making falling into the sea only a 1 HP penalty after watching Zayna play.
